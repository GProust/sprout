# Sprout Trademark & Branding Policy

The **source code** of Sprout is free software, licensed under the
[GNU GPLv3](LICENSE). **Forking, improving and contributing to Sprout is
welcome and encouraged.** This document is only about the **name and icon** —
and exists solely to stop user-deceiving copycats, not to get in the way of
good-faith forks or contributors.

## In short

- The **code** is yours to use, study, modify, redistribute and contribute back
  under the GPLv3. Nothing here restricts that.
- The only ask: if you **publish a build to other people**, don't present it
  under the "Sprout" name or icon in a way that could make users think it is the
  official app. Use your own name and icon for a public release.

## You are free to do this — no permission needed

- **Fork the project and change anything you like.** Improve it, experiment,
  fix bugs, add features.
- Build and share your modified version for personal, family or testing use.
- Open pull requests and contribute your improvements back, under the project's
  own name.
- Describe your work truthfully — e.g. "based on Sprout", "a fork of Sprout",
  "compatible with Sprout".

## Please ask first before doing this

- **Publishing** an app (e.g. to Google Play or another store) under the
  "Sprout" name or icon — or a confusingly similar one — that users could
  mistake for the official Sprout.
- Using the Sprout icon or logo as the icon/logo of your own published app or
  service.

So: **fork and improve freely; just pick your own name and icon if you publish
your version to the public.** (A redistributed, modified version must also stay
open source under the GPLv3 — see [LICENSE](LICENSE).)

## Why this exists

Sprout is a private health tracker for parents and newborns. Keeping the name
and icon distinct is only to protect users from copycat or malicious apps
impersonating the official, source-available app — reporting an impersonating
app to a store relies on these reserved marks. It is **not** meant to discourage
anyone from forking or improving the app.
